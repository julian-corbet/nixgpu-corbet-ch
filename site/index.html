nixgpu

Share one GPU by priority, not by budget

One physical card, many consumers: k8s pods, an LLM server, an image generator, a video transcoder, and your desktop — co-residing when they fit, yielding by priority when they don't. For GPUs with in-kernel drivers (AMD today; Intel structurally compatible; NVIDIA excluded until an in-tree stack matures).

The mechanism

  • Co-reside whatever fits. Nothing is declared, capped, or reserved — decisions use live measured VRAM.
  • Priority decides who leaves. Compute VRAM is pinned — the kernel cannot evict it — so reclaim means scaling the lowest-priority tenant to zero. A small reactive watcher does exactly that, and nothing else.
  • The desktop is just the top tenant. Its graphics VRAM spills to GTT under pressure; the watcher reads the spill from sysfs and sheds k8s tenants lowest-first. No host agent, no reserved floor.
  • Video is separate silicon. The media engine gets its own scheduling lane and is never evicted for compute pressure.
  • The user is always told what is happening. Cold start, contention, desktop-in-use — one honest waiting page, never a silent hang.

Status

Pre-alpha. The mechanisms are proven in production on a 16 GiB RDNA2 card; this repo is their generalization, and the extraction has not started. The first real artifact is the behavior contract the platform is built and tested against. Nothing advertised here is invented — but nothing is packaged yet either.

Interoperating set

nixgpu targets a nixidy-rendered, Argo CD-synced cluster — the spine shipped by the sibling nixk3s. Tenant apps that consume the GPU contract live in nixapps.
